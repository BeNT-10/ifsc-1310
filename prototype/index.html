Operation Paul Bunyan

The Tree That Almost Caused WWIII

Preface

Tensions at Korea's DMZ were still high far after the Korean War "ended." These tensions came to a head during the summer of 1976, when South Korea decided that a 100 foot tall tree in the middle of the DMZ needed to be removed because it obscured a North Korean observation post.

North Korea, however, didn't like that idea.

When 5 South Korean soldiers and 11 American soldiers went to trim the tree, about 40 North Korean soldiers were sent to stop them. After the North Korean soliders observed the South Koreans and Americans for about 15 minutes, they abruptly ordered them to stop trimming the tree. The South Korean and American soldiers refused to comply with these orders, and the North Koreans reacted by attacking and killing one American, which caused the rest of the soldiers to flee the scene. The fight lasted about 20 seconds.

The Beginning of Operation Paul Bunyan

The sitting United States president at that time, President Gerald Ford, was immediately contacted and informed about the situation. After convening with South Korean leaders, the plan Operation Paul Bunyan was created - it's mission?

Cut down that tree.

The Suit-Up Phase

On August 21st, three days after the killing, the United States and South Korea prepared to execute Operation Paul Bunyan in the following manner:

  • The bridge North Korean soldiers would have crossed to confront the Americans and South Koreans was armed with bombs ready to detonate at a moment's notice
  • A gun used for demolishing structures was loaded and aimed at the bridge to ensure that if for whatever reason the bombs failed, the bridge could still be destroyed.
  • Sixty four members of the South Korean Special Forces strapped claymores to their chests, armed themselves with rifles and grenade launchers, set up a sandbag barricade, and dared the North Korean soldiers on the other side to cross the bridge.
  • A U.S. Infantry company circled the area in utility helicopters.
  • Seven Cobra attack helicopters escorted the utility helicopters.
  • Multiple B-52 Stratofortresses circled the area.
  • Fighter jets from four different air bases accompanied the B-52s.
  • The U.S. aircraft carrier USS Midway was deployed to the area.
  • DEFCON 2 was declared by the order of a U.S. General and 12,000 troops were deployed to the surrounding area.
  • Nuclear capable bombers were placed on standby.
  • A nearby Japanese airbase was lined with Lockheed C-130s ready to take off at a moment's notice.
